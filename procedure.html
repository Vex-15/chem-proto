Synthesis Protocol

Step 1: Preparation

Clean all glassware (beakers, measuring cylinders) thoroughly. Ensure the workspace is well-ventilated and free from open flames.

Step 2: Mixing Alcohol

Measure 75.15 ml of Isopropyl alcohol into a clean 100 mL graduated flask or beaker.

Step 3: Adding Peroxide

Add 4.17 ml of Hydrogen Peroxide using a measuring cylinder. Stir gently.

Step 4: Adding Glycerol

Add 1.45 ml of Glycerol. Since glycerol is viscous, rinse the measuring cylinder with sterile water to ensure all of it enters the mixture.

Step 5: Final Dilution

Top up the flask with sterile distilled water until the total volume reaches exactly 100 ml. Cap and mix by gentle inversion.

  • Flammability: Isopropyl alcohol is highly flammable. Keep away from heat sources.
  • Eye Protection: Wear safety goggles to prevent splashes, especially from Hydrogen Peroxide.
  • Inhalation: Work in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling strong vapors.
  • Ingestion: The final product is for external use only. Label bottles clearly "Do Not Drink".

Post-Production: Quarantine

Once bottled, the solution should be kept in quarantine for 72 hours. This allows time for any spores present in the alcohol or the new bottles to be destroyed by the Hydrogen Peroxide.
