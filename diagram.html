The following visual documentation illustrates the sequential stages and system architecture involved in the hand sanitizer formulation process.

[Image of hand sanitizer manufacturing process flow chart]

Figure 1.1: Comprehensive System Block Diagram and Chemical Process Flow

Stage 1: Input

Collection and precise measurement of raw reagents (Alcohol, H2O2, Glycerol) based on stoichiometric calculations for a 100 mL batch.

Stage 2: Processing

Controlled mixing in a sterile vessel. The order of addition is critical to ensure proper solubility and to prevent adverse chemical reactions.

Stage 3: Output

Transfer to dispensing units followed by a mandatory 72-hour quarantine period to ensure the final prototype is spore-free.
