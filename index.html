Alcohol-Based Hand Sanitizer

Academic Prototype for Chemical Engineering & Public Health

Project Abstract

This prototype demonstrates the laboratory-scale formulation of a WHO-recommended hand rub. The project focuses on optimizing the chemical balance between efficacy (antimicrobial action) and skin safety (humectant properties).


Using Isopropyl Alcohol as the active agent, the solution is designed to denature proteins in bacteria and viruses, providing a rapid disinfection mechanism for public health safety.

Objectives

  • Formulation: To synthesize a stable disinfectant using 75% Isopropyl Alcohol.
  • Safety: To analyze the role of Hydrogen Peroxide in eliminating spores.
  • Cost-Efficiency: To demonstrate a cost-effective alternative to commercial gels.
  • Compliance: To adhere strictly to World Health Organization (WHO) safety protocols.
